What is Darien?

Darien is the largest and most unpopulated province in Panama and it is approximately five hours away from Panama City. It is mostly inhabited by indigenous populations (the Wargandi, the Embera Wounnan, and the Guna). Because of its dense forests and swamps, the eastern part of the Darien province remains almost untouched by the realm of technology. All the pictures displayed on this website were taken by Rafael and try to capture the life of the Embera Wounnan culture.

While there are many tribes of Embera Wounnan dispersed through the whole Darien region, most of them have been absorbed by the Western culture and have left behind their true traditions. The pictures shown were all taken in Laurel, a small Embera village that is composed by less than 40 people, and one of the most authentic that still remain nowadays. Even though most members of the tribe speak Spanish, they communicate among themselves in Embera, their own language, that is hundreds of years old and that dates to the times before the Spanish colonization. To survive the Embera hunt, harverst, and fish. It is one of the few tribes of the Embera that has escaped the civilized world and will remain as one of the few ones in the future if we do not support their culture and life style.
